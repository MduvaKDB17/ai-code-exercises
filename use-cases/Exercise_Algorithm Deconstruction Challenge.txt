1. How did the AI's explanation change your understanding of the algorithm?

The AI explanation helped me understand that the algorithm does not simply sort tasks by priority. It calculates a score using several 
factors such as task priority, due dates, status, tags, and recent updates. This showed me how multiple conditions work together to 
determine task importance.

2. What aspects were still difficult to understand after AI explanation?

The most difficult part was understanding how the different score adjustments combine to produce the final score. It was also challenging
to follow how positive and negative values affect the final ranking of tasks.

3. How would you explain this algorithm to another junior developer?

I would explain that the algorithm assigns a score to each task based on its priority, due date, status, tags, and update history. 
Tasks with higher scores are considered more important. After calculating scores for all tasks, the algorithm sorts them from highest 
to lowest score so that the most important tasks appear first.

4. Did you test this understanding against AI?

Yes. I tested my understanding by providing different task examples and checking whether the AI calculated the correct scores and 
task order. The results matched the algorithm's logic and confirmed my understanding.

5. How might you improve the algorithm based on your understanding?

The algorithm could be improved by allowing users to customize score weights, adding support for recurring tasks, considering task 
complexity, and using machine learning to learn user preferences. Performance could also be improved by caching previously calculated 
scores.

Insights and Learning Points
Priority alone is not enough to determine task importance.
Due dates significantly influence task urgency.
Completed tasks should receive lower scores.
Tags can be used to identify critical tasks.
Weighted scoring provides more accurate prioritization.
Sorting algorithms become more useful when combined with business rules.

Conclusion
The Task Priority Sorting Algorithm is an effective method for ranking tasks according to their importance. By combining priority 
levels, due dates, task status, tags, and update history, it creates a more intelligent prioritization system. This exercise improved 
my understanding of weighted scoring systems, sorting algorithms, and practical task management applications.
